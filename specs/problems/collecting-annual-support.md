# Problem: Collecting annual support is manual and leaky

**Status:** Draft
**Owner:** Small organizations (marching bands, choirs, sports teams, community groups)

## Who has this problem
Small, often volunteer-run organizations that want financial backing from their
local community, and the supporters who would gladly pay a small yearly amount if
it were easy.

## The problem
There is no simple, dedicated way for a small organization to collect an annual
supporting-member fee. Today they improvise with bank transfers, cash, invoices,
spreadsheets, or generic payment links. Supporters don't know how to pay or how
much; organizers chase payments by hand; money and goodwill leak through the
cracks. The effort is disproportionate to the small sums involved, so many
organizations never collect support at all.

## Why it matters
For the organization, supporting-member income is meaningful funding that is left
on the table. For the supporter, the friction means a willing contribution never
happens. For the product, this friction is the entire reason to exist.

## What "solved" looks like
- An organization can stand up a way to accept supporting members in minutes,
  with no custom payment plumbing.
- A supporter can go from "I want to support" to "I have paid this year" in a
  single, obvious flow.
- Money collected is attributable to a named supporter without manual
  reconciliation.
