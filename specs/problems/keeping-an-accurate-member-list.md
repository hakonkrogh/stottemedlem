# Problem: Keeping an accurate list of who has paid

**Status:** Draft
**Owner:** Small organizations and their administrators

## Who has this problem
The person who runs an organization's membership — typically a volunteer
treasurer or secretary — who needs to know, at any moment, who is currently a
paid-up supporting member.

## The problem
Even when fees are collected, the *list* of supporting members drifts out of
date. Payments live in a bank statement, names live in a spreadsheet, and the two
are reconciled by hand, late and imperfectly. It is hard to answer basic
questions: Who is a member right now? Who has lapsed and should be reminded? How
many supporters do we have this year? Memberships are annual, so the list also
silently goes stale as the year turns.

## Why it matters
An inaccurate list means lost renewals, awkward mistakes (treating a lapsed
person as current, or vice versa), and no trustworthy count to report to a board
or grant application. The organization can't act on support it can't see.

## What "solved" looks like
- The organization has one authoritative, always-current list of supporting
  members and each member's status (active or lapsed).
- Status follows directly from whether the annual fee is paid for the current
  period — no manual reconciliation.
- Lapsed members are visible so they can be invited to renew.
